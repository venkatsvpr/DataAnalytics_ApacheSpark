By MAÏA de la BAUMEMARCH 13, 2015
PARIS — French judges on Friday placed under formal investigation two men believed to have links to Amedy Coulibaly, one of the three gunmen involved in the terrorist attacks in and around Paris in January, the Paris prosecutor’s office said in a statement.The two men, identified by prosecutors as Amar R., 33, and Said M., 25, are charged with “terrorist conspiracy to commit crimes against people.”They are part of a group of four people who were detained this week, including a police officer described by news reports as Amar’s girlfriend. Police officers freed the two other detainees, and according to the news reports one of them was the officer.Mr. Coulibaly, 32, a French citizen with immigrant roots, died in a police assault on a kosher grocery on Jan. 9 after he took several hostages, killing four of them, and a day after he shot a police officer in Montrouge, a Paris suburb.
