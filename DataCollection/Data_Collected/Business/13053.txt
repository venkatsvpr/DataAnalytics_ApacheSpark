Its rapid growth in stores in the years leading up to the 2008 financial crisis has since weighed on PacSun’s bottom line.PacSun, which once dressed teenagers in brands like Billabong and Quiksilver, has tried to refashion itself as edgier streetwear retailer.Still, the retailer said it lost $10 million in the fourth quarter, compared with a net loss of $26 million over the same period the previous year. It said sales at stores open for at least a year edged up 0.2 percent in the fourth quarter. But for the full year, sales dipped 2.6 percent.For now, PacSun said its 601 stores would continue to operate without interruption to customers, employees or vendors.The retailer, which is based in Anaheim, Ca., filed for Chapter 11 in the federal bankruptcy court in Delaware early Thursday.
