By CHAD BRAYAPRIL 25, 2016
LONDON — GE Money Bank, a unit of GE Capital in the Czech Republic, said on Monday that its initial public offering could value the banking business at 39.1 billion Czech korunas, or about $1.6 billion.The initial public offering is the latest in a series of moves by General Electric as it seeks to retreat from finance and focus on its industrial roots.General Electric said last year that it planned to sell the bulk of GE Capital within two years. Since then, GE Capital has entered into agreements for asset sales worth about $165 billion.The Czech business, which will be renamed Moneta Money Bank, said it planned to price its offering on the Prague Stock Exchange at 68 korunas to 85 korunas a share. That would value the bank at 39.1 billion korunas at the midpoint of the expected range.
