The technology itself uses low-powered television channels to cover long distances and can penetrate obstacles like concrete walls.Microsoft said its new plan would help rural Americans — many of whom live in remote areas that haven’t had fast internet service — get exactly that kind of service. The company is positioning white space technology as a potential way to plug the digital divide.There are, of course, a few problems with the plan. The hurdles include a lack of devices that can work on white space technology and opposition from television broadcasters that worry that their own broadcasts may face interference if people are getting online through unused television channels.Microsoft seems undeterred.
