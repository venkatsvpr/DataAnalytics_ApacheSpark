Partnership structures at law firms hinder investment in longer-term litigation, which can require costly improvements in technology or data security. Corporations trying to handle large-scale litigation are also hamstrung by tighter budgets, the general counsels said in the survey.To gain more financial flexibility, law firms have been using outside lenders. Last year, Burford committed $30 million to the Hausfeld law firm to expand its presence in Germany, for example. Burford, based in New York, also said early this year that it was committing $100 million in financing for a group of cases at a large, unnamed law firm. Typically, law firms are private entities and disclose little about their financing arrangements.Burford was founded in 2009 by Christopher P.
